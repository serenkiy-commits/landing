📢 1. Marketing & Advertising

1.1

Unique Value Proposition Generator

I sell [your product] to [target audience] in the [niche] space. My main competitors are: [competitor 1], [competitor 2], [competitor 3]. Analyze their positioning and suggest 5 unique value proposition options that clearly differentiate me from them. For each option, include: the UVP as a single sentence, which pain point it addresses, and why competitors can't easily copy it. Format: table.

💡 Tip: Use this after researching competitor websites so the data is specific and grounded.

1.2

Ad Creatives for Paid Social

I need 10 ad creative variations for [your product] on [platform: Facebook/Instagram/LinkedIn/TikTok]. Target audience: [target audience], primary pain point: [customer pain point]. For each creative, write: headline (under 40 characters), primary text (under 125 characters), visual description, call to action. Use different approaches: pain/solution, social proof, urgency, benefit, curiosity. Don't repeat the same structure.

💡 Tip: Run A/B tests with the top 3 variants, then scale the winner.

1.3

Monthly Media Plan

Create a media plan for [time period] to promote [your product]. Budget: [budget]. Target audience: [target audience]. Region: [region]. Split the budget across channels (search ads, paid social, influencers, content marketing) with reasoning for each allocation. For each channel, specify: goal, KPI, ad format, expected CPL, and number of leads. Present the final table on a week-by-week basis.

💡 Tip: Adjust the plan every 2 weeks based on actual cost-per-lead data.

💼 2. Sales & Negotiations

2.1

Cold Call Script

Write a cold call script for selling [your product] to a decision-maker: [DM title]. Client's company: [company type]. Key pain point: [customer pain point]. The script should include: getting past the gatekeeper (2 approaches), greeting and rapport (15 seconds), qualifying question, one-sentence benefit pitch, handling 5 common objections, closing for the next step. Mark pauses and tone shifts.

💡 Tip: Read the script aloud and time it. An ideal cold call runs 2-3 minutes.

2.2

Objection Handling

Compile the 15 most common objections when selling [your product] to [target audience]. For each objection, provide 3 response options using different techniques: acknowledge and reframe, redirect question, case study/proof. Objections should cover: price, timing, trust, need, competitors, and stalling. Format: table with columns "Objection / Technique / Response."

💡 Tip: Memorize one best response for each objection until it becomes second nature.

2.3

Business Proposal

Write a business proposal for [your product/service] for [company name or type]. Decision-maker: [title]. Their challenge: [what they want to solve]. Client budget: [if known]. Structure: one-page executive summary (why us, what we offer, expected result), solution description, phases and timeline, pricing (3 tiers), case studies (2-3 relevant ones), guarantees, next step. Tone: professional but jargon-free. Length: 3-4 pages.

💡 Tip: Tailor the case studies section to the specific client's industry.

✍️ 3. Content & Copywriting

3.1

Monthly Content Plan

Create a 30-day content plan for [brand/product] in the [niche] space. Target audience: [target audience]. Content goal: [attract/engage/sell]. Channels: [blog/social media/newsletter]. For each day: topic, format (article/post/video/carousel), key message, CTA, hashtags (if social). Content mix: 40% educational, 30% engaging, 20% promotional, 10% entertaining. Topics should not repeat.

💡 Tip: Batch your content: create an entire week's content in a single session.

3.2

Long-Form Blog Post

Write the structure and full text of a blog article on the topic "[article topic]." Target keyword: [SEO keyword]. Target audience: [target audience]. Length: 2,000-3,000 words. Structure: H1 headline (with keyword), introduction with a hook, 5-7 H2 sections with H3 subsections, practical examples in each section, conclusion with CTA. Tone: authoritative yet accessible. Insert 3 spots for internal links and 2 for external authoritative sources.

💡 Tip: Start with the most valuable section so readers get value even if they only skim.

3.3

AIDA Sales Copy

Write sales copy for [your product] using the AIDA formula. Target audience: [target audience]. Primary pain point: [customer pain point]. Result the customer gets: [result]. Price: $[price]. Attention: provocative headline and opening paragraph. Interest: unpack the problem so readers see themselves (3 scenarios). Desire: show the solution and results (facts, numbers, case study). Action: offer with a deadline and bonus. Length: 1,500-2,000 words.

💡 Tip: Read the copy aloud. If you stumble on a phrase, rewrite it simpler.

🔍 4. SEO & Traffic

4.1

SEO Strategy for a New Site

Build a 6-month SEO strategy for a new website in the [niche] space. Domain: [new/existing with history]. Region: [region]. Budget: [budget]. Include: technical audit (15-point checklist), keyword research strategy, page prioritization for ranking, monthly link-building plan, blog content plan (SEO articles), monthly KPIs (rankings, traffic, conversions). Show realistic traffic growth expectations.

💡 Tip: For the first 3 months, focus on low-competition keywords with high purchase intent.

4.2

Keyword Clustering

Here is a list of keywords for [your product/website]: [paste 30-50 keywords]. Cluster them by intent: informational, navigational, commercial, transactional. For each cluster: assign a target page (existing or new), priority (high/medium/low), recommended content type, search volume (estimate if data not provided). Identify overlap clusters where one piece of content can cover multiple groups.

💡 Tip: Start with transactional and commercial clusters first. They drive revenue.

4.3

Technical SEO

Run a technical SEO audit for [website]. Create a 30-point checklist covering: page speed (Core Web Vitals), mobile experience, indexing (robots.txt, sitemap, canonicals), URL structure, internal linking, duplicate content, broken links, structured data (Schema.org), security (HTTPS, mixed content), international SEO (hreflang). For each item: what to check, which tool to use, pass/fail criteria, fix priority.

💡 Tip: Fix technical issues before investing in content and links.

📧 5. Email Marketing

5.1

Welcome Series

Write a 5-email welcome series for new subscribers of [brand/product]. Lead magnet: [what the subscriber received]. Target audience: [target audience]. Series goal: [sell/engage/educate]. For each email: subject line (under 50 characters), preheader, body (300-500 words), CTA, send day (from signup). Email 1: deliver lead magnet + introduction. Email 2: story/expertise. Email 3: case study. Email 4: FAQ. Email 5: offer.

💡 Tip: The first email should go out within 5 minutes of subscribing.

5.2

Re-Engagement Series

Write a 4-email re-engagement series for inactive subscribers of [brand]. Inactivity period: [number] days. Segment size: [number] people. Average order value: $[amount]. Email 1: "We miss you" (no discount, just value). Email 2: "Here's what you've missed" (news/updates). Email 3: "Special offer" (deal with a deadline). Email 4: "Last chance" (unsubscribe or confirm). For each: subject, body, timing. Specify when to remove inactive contacts from the list.

💡 Tip: Cleaning inactive subscribers from your list improves deliverability and lowers your ESP costs.

5.3

Launch Sales Sequence

Write a 7-email sales sequence for launching [your product]. Target audience: [target audience]. Price: $[price]. Deadline: [date]. Structure: day 1 (problem story), day 2 (common mistakes your audience makes), day 3 (new approach), day 4 (customer case study), day 5 (product details + offer), day 6 (objection handling), day 7 (last chance). For each email: catchy subject line, preheader, full body, P.S. with an extra trigger.

💡 Tip: The P.S. after the signature gets read more often than the body. Put your key message there.

📱 6. Social Media (SMM)

6.1

SMM Strategy

Develop a 3-month social media strategy for [brand] in the [niche] space. Current state: [number] followers, [number] reach, [number]% engagement rate. Goals: [follower growth/sales/awareness]. Platforms: [social networks]. Include: positioning on each platform, content mix (ratio of content types), visual style (description), posting frequency, hashtag strategy, comment engagement plan, promotion budget, monthly KPIs.

💡 Tip: It's better to do 2 platforms well than 5 poorly.

6.2

Reels and Shorts

Write 10 short video scripts (Reels/Shorts/TikTok) for [brand/product]. Target audience: [target audience]. Format: 15-60 seconds. For each video: hook (first 3 seconds), second-by-second script, on-screen text, audio/music, CTA, hashtags. Types: educational (3), entertaining (2), promotional (2), trend-based (2), behind-the-scenes (1). Every hook should stop the scroll.

💡 Tip: Film 5 different hooks for each video and pick the one with the best 3-second retention.

6.3

Telegram Channel Content

Create a 2-week content plan for the Telegram channel [brand/topic] with [number] subscribers. Topic: [niche]. For each day (1-2 posts): post type (opinion/breakdown/news/tool/poll/case study), full post text (200-400 words), formatting (bold, italic, links), publish time. Account for Telegram specifics: long-form text is fine, links are clickable, polls are native. Tone: [describe tone].

💡 Tip: Telegram posts at 8:00-9:00 AM and 7:00-9:00 PM get maximum reach.

📊 7. Analytics & Strategy

7.1

Business Model Canvas

Fill in a Business Model Canvas for [your product/company]. For each of the 9 blocks, provide a detailed description: key partners (who and why), key activities (what we do), key resources (what we need), value proposition (what we deliver), customer relationships (how we interact), channels (how we deliver value), customer segments (who we sell to), cost structure (where money goes), revenue streams (where money comes from). After filling it in, identify 3 weak spots in the model and how to strengthen them.

💡 Tip: Revisit the Canvas every quarter. The business model should evolve.

7.2

SWOT Analysis

Conduct a detailed SWOT analysis for [your product/company] in the [niche] space. For each quadrant, provide at least 5 items with explanations: Strengths (internal), Weaknesses (internal), Opportunities (external), Threats (external). After the analysis, build a strategy matrix: SO (use strengths to capture opportunities), WO (use opportunities to offset weaknesses), ST (use strengths to defend against threats), WT (minimize weaknesses and threats). Prioritize the strategies.

💡 Tip: A SWOT is only useful if it leads to concrete actions. Don't leave it as theory.

7.3

Unit Economics Analysis

Calculate unit economics for [your product]. Inputs: product price $[price], COGS $[amount], monthly ad spend $[amount], leads/month [number], conversion rate [number]%, average purchase frequency [number times/year], average customer lifespan [number months]. Calculate: CAC, LTV, LTV/CAC, margin, breakeven point, payback period. Flag which metrics are healthy and which need improvement. Suggest 3 levers to improve unit economics.

💡 Tip: LTV/CAC below 3 means the business model needs adjustment.

Business Process Automation Map

Analyze the business processes at [your business/company] and identify what can be automated. Processes: [list 5-10 core processes]. For each process, assess: current time cost (hours/month), automation potential (high/medium/low), automation tools (specific services), implementation cost, expected time and money savings, priority (by ROI). Build a 6-month automation roadmap.

💡 Tip: Start with processes that repeat daily and don't require creative judgment.

8.2

Sales Chatbot

Design a chatbot for qualifying and selling [your product]. Platform: [website/WhatsApp/Facebook Messenger]. Target audience: [target audience]. Describe: dialogue tree (from first message to handoff to a rep or purchase), copy for each bot message, branching conditions, integrations (CRM, payment system), FAQ database (15 questions with answers), escalation scenario to a live agent, bot performance metrics.

For each: flow diagram, required integrations, setup time.

💡 Tip: Automate what already works manually first. Don't invent new processes.

🎯 9. Customer Service

9.1

Service Standards

Develop customer service standards for [your business]. Channels: [list: phone, email, chat, messaging, social media]. For each channel: SLA for response time (first response and resolution), communication tone (with sample phrases), templates for common situations (5), escalation (when and to whom), quality metrics. General rules: 10 "always do" items and 10 "never do" items. Quality control system: how to audit compliance.

💡 Tip: Standards only work if they're regularly audited and updated.

9.2

Support Scripts

Write 15 scripts for the support team at [your product]. Inquiry categories: technical issues (5), billing questions (3), returns and exchanges (3), complaints (2), compliments (2). For each script: trigger (what the customer contacts about), response text (adaptable), agent actions (what to check, what to do), resolution time (SLA), escalation conditions. Tone: [friendly/professional/neutral]. Each script should be a template, not a rigid text.

💡 Tip: Give agents the freedom to adapt scripts to the situation. Robotic responses irritate customers.

9.3

Complaint Handling System

Develop a complaint handling process for [your business]. Current complaint volume: [number]/month. Main causes: [list]. Process: intake (how we log), classification (categories and priorities), investigation (who and how), resolution (compensation options), customer communication (copy for each stage), closure (satisfaction confirmation), analysis (root causes). For each stage: owner, SLA, tool. Metrics: FCR, CSAT on complaints, resolution time.

💡 Tip: Every complaint is a gift. It shows what's broken in the process.

💰 10. Finance & Business Planning

10.1

Business Plan

Write a business plan for [your product/business]. Stage: [idea/launch/growth]. Starting capital: $[amount]. Niche: [niche]. Region: [region]. Structure: executive summary (1 page), business description, market analysis, product/service, marketing and sales, operations plan, team, financial plan (revenue, expenses, P&L for 3 years), investment (what's needed, sources), risks and mitigation. For the financial plan: monthly detail for year 1, quarterly for years 2 and 3.

💡 Tip: Investors read the financial plan from the back. Start with when you hit breakeven.

10.2

Financial Model

Build a financial model for [your product/business]. Inputs: product price $[price], COGS $[amount], fixed costs/month $[amount], variable costs/unit $[amount], customer acquisition cost $[amount], conversion rate [number]%, retention rate [number]%/month. Calculate for 12 months: revenue, gross profit, net profit, breakeven point, cumulative cash flow, runway at current burn. Show 3 scenarios: optimistic, base, pessimistic. State key assumptions.

💡 Tip: A financial model is useful not for forecast accuracy, but for understanding the key levers.

10.3

Pricing Strategy

Develop a pricing strategy for [your product]. COGS: $[amount]. Target margin: [number]%. Competitors: [competitor 1: $price], [competitor 2: $price]. Target audience: [description, willingness to pay]. Analyze strategies: cost-plus, value-based, competitive, freemium, tiered pricing. Recommend the optimal model with rationale. Design 3 pricing tiers: names, what's included in each, price, who it's for. Pricing psychology: justify the specific numbers chosen.

💡 Tip: The middle tier should be the "obviously best choice" (decoy effect).
