1. Marketing & Advertising

1.1

Unique Value Proposition Generator

I sell [your product] to [target audience] in the [niche] space. My main competitors are: [competitor 1], [competitor 2], [competitor 3]. Analyze their positioning and suggest 5 unique value proposition options that clearly differentiate me from them. For each option, include: the UVP as a single sentence, which pain point it addresses, and why competitors can't easily copy it. Format: table.

💡 Tip: Use this after researching competitor websites so the data is specific and grounded.

1.2

Ad Creatives for Paid Social

I need 10 ad creative variations for [your product] on [platform: Facebook/Instagram/LinkedIn/TikTok]. Target audience: [target audience], primary pain point: [customer pain point]. For each creative, write: headline (under 40 characters), primary text (under 125 characters), visual description, call to action. Use different approaches: pain/solution, social proof, urgency, benefit, curiosity. Don't repeat the same structure.

💡 Tip: Run A/B tests with the top 3 variants, then scale the winner.

1.3

Monthly Media Plan

Create a media plan for [time period] to promote [your product]. Budget: [budget]. Target audience: [target audience]. Region: [region]. Split the budget across channels (search ads, paid social, influencers, content marketing) with reasoning for each allocation. For each channel, specify: goal, KPI, ad format, expected CPL, and number of leads. Present the final table on a week-by-week basis.

💡 Tip: Adjust the plan every 2 weeks based on actual cost-per-lead data.

1.4

Ad Campaign Analysis

Here are my ad campaign metrics: impressions [number], clicks [number], CTR [number]%, conversions [number], CPA $[amount], total spend $[amount]. Product: [your product], channel: [channel], audience: [target audience]. Analyze these metrics. Flag which ones fall below benchmarks for the [niche] industry. Give specific recommendations to improve each weak metric. Suggest 3 hypotheses for the next test.

💡 Tip: Plug in real numbers from your ads manager for an accurate analysis.

1.5

New Market Entry Strategy

I want to launch [your product] in the [target region/country] market. Current market: [current region]. Target audience in the new market: [target audience]. Build a step-by-step market entry strategy: preparation phases, product and messaging adaptation, channel selection, test budget, and success criteria for scaling decisions. Account for cultural and language differences in the region.

💡 Tip: Start with a minimal test on one channel before committing the full budget.

1.6

Affiliate Program Design

Design an affiliate program structure for [your product]. Target affiliates: [type of partners, e.g., influencers, agencies, freelancers]. Average order value: $[amount]. Describe: compensation model (% of sales, flat fee, hybrid), payout terms, affiliate tiers, materials for affiliates (what to provide), tracking system, program KPIs. Include a unit economics calculation for one affiliate.

💡 Tip: Test the terms with 5-10 affiliates before publicly launching the program.

1.7

Brand Positioning

Help me rethink the positioning of [brand]. Current situation: [describe current brand perception]. Target audience: [target audience]. Niche: [niche]. Competitors: [competitor 1], [competitor 2]. Formulate: Brand Positioning Statement, core brand message, tone of voice with examples, 5 communication pillars with explanations, and what we are NOT (anti-positioning). Everything should be actionable starting tomorrow.

💡 Tip: Test the new positioning with a focus group of 5-7 people from your target audience.

1.8

Promotional Campaigns

Come up with 7 promotional campaigns for [your product] during [time period/event]. Target audience: [target audience]. Average order value: $[amount]. For each campaign, specify: mechanics, duration, announcement channels, expected sales lift in %, margin calculation including the discount, risks and how to mitigate them. Don't suggest generic 10-20% discounts without a creative mechanic.

💡 Tip: Pick the 2 campaigns with the best margin-to-expected-lift ratio.

1.9

Marketing Funnel

Design a marketing funnel for [your product] from first touch to repeat purchase. Target audience: [target audience]. Traffic channels: [channels]. For each stage (awareness, interest, decision, purchase, retention), specify: key user action, content/offer, tool/channel, metric, and conversion benchmark for the [niche] industry. Create a summary table with unit economics projections.

💡 Tip: Build the funnel from the bottom up, starting with purchase and working back to first touch.

1.10

Landing Page Copy

Write the complete copy structure for a landing page for [your product]. Target audience: [target audience]. Primary pain point: [customer pain point]. Result the customer gets: [result]. Structure: headline using the 4U formula, subheadline, problem block (3 pain points), solution, how it works (3-5 steps), benefits (6 items), social proof (what to request from customers), FAQ (5 questions with answers), final CTA. Write each block as ready-to-use copy.

💡 Tip: Test different headlines using Google Optimize or a similar tool to find the best-converting version.

1.11

Referral Mechanics

Design a referral program for [your product]. Current customers: [number]. Average LTV: $[amount]. Describe: what the referrer gets, what the referred person gets, bonus activation conditions, tracking mechanics, communication scripts (messages/emails to the referrer), virality forecast (k-factor), and cost of acquisition via referral vs current CAC.

💡 Tip: A two-sided bonus (for both referrer and referred) outperforms one-sided incentives.

1.12

Communications Rebrand

Audit the current advertising messages for [brand] and propose updates. Here are examples of current messages: [paste 3-5 examples]. Target audience: [target audience]. What's not working: [problem]. For each message: identify weaknesses, rewrite in the new style, explain the logic behind changes. At the end, provide 5-7 brand communication rules with "do this / not this" examples.

💡 Tip: Document the rules in a style guide and share with everyone who writes on behalf of the brand.

1.13

Brand Collaborations

Suggest 10 collaboration ideas for [brand] with other brands or influencers. Niche: [niche]. Target audience: [target audience]. Brand values: [values]. For each idea, specify: collaboration partner, format (co-branded product, content, event), what each side gets, approximate budget, KPI. Sort by cost-to-potential-reach ratio.

💡 Tip: Start with micro-collaborations to test the format before making large investments.

1.14

Seasonal Marketing Calendar

Build a 12-month marketing calendar for [your product] in the [niche] space. For each month, specify: key events and holidays, campaign theme, primary channel, offer type, budget priority (high/medium/low). Account for seasonal demand patterns in the niche. Separately highlight the 3 peak months and the revenue maximization strategy for each.

💡 Tip: Prepare materials for peak months at least 6 weeks before launch.

1.15

Price Positioning

Help me determine the optimal price for [your product]. Cost of goods: $[amount]. Competitor prices: [competitor 1] — $[price], [competitor 2] — $[price], [competitor 3] — $[price]. Target audience: [target audience]. Analyze: competitor price positioning, perceived value of my product, optimal price for a [penetration/premium/mid-market] strategy. Propose 3 pricing tiers with different feature sets and justify the pricing psychology.

💡 Tip: Test pricing on small traffic volumes via A/B testing on the landing page.

1.16

B2B Marketing

Develop a B2B customer acquisition strategy for [your product]. Target companies: [size, industry, region]. Decision-maker: [title]. Sales cycle: [duration]. Average deal size: $[amount]. Describe: lead generation channels (at least 5), content strategy for each funnel stage, ABM (account-based marketing) approach for the top 20 target accounts, lead qualification system, KPIs per channel.

💡 Tip: In B2B, relationships and thought leadership content outperform ad spend.

1.17

Crisis Marketing

Situation: [describe crisis, e.g., 40% sales drop, negative PR, loss of a key channel]. Product: [your product]. Current budget: [budget]. Build a crisis marketing plan for [time period]: immediate actions (first 48 hours), short-term measures (2 weeks), mid-term strategy (1-3 months). For each action: what we do, who's responsible, expected result, cost.

💡 Tip: In a crisis, focus on retaining existing customers rather than acquiring new ones.

1.18

Offline Marketing

Propose an offline marketing strategy for [your product] in [region]. Budget: [budget]. Target audience: [target audience]. Places where the audience congregates: [list]. Consider: outdoor advertising, events, street teams, partnerships with local businesses, POS materials, sampling. For each channel: format, cost, reach, how to measure effectiveness. How to connect offline with the online funnel.

💡 Tip: Always add a QR code or promo code to track offline touchpoints.

1.19

Marketing Brief

Create a detailed marketing brief for an agency/freelancer to promote [your product]. Include: company and product description, goals and KPIs, target audience (segments with descriptions), competitors, current situation (what's been done, results), budget and timeline, constraints and requirements, evaluation criteria. The brief should be self-contained: anyone reading it should understand the task without asking additional questions.

💡 Tip: A good brief saves 30-50% of time on approvals and revisions.

1.20

Retargeting Strategy

Develop a retargeting strategy for [your product]. Website traffic: [number] visitors/month. Conversion rate: [number]%. Average order value: $[amount]. Segment the retargeting audience: visitors without action, cart abandoners, past customers, lookalike audiences. For each segment: ad message, offer, display frequency, audience lifespan, budget. Calculate the expected ROAS for each segment.

💡 Tip: Exclude recent buyers from retargeting to avoid wasting budget.

2. Sales & Negotiations

2.1

Cold Call Script

Write a cold call script for selling [your product] to a decision-maker: [DM title]. Client's company: [company type]. Key pain point: [customer pain point]. The script should include: getting past the gatekeeper (2 approaches), greeting and rapport (15 seconds), qualifying question, one-sentence benefit pitch, handling 5 common objections, closing for the next step. Mark pauses and tone shifts.

💡 Tip: Read the script aloud and time it. An ideal cold call runs 2-3 minutes.

2.2

Objection Handling

Compile the 15 most common objections when selling [your product] to [target audience]. For each objection, provide 3 response options using different techniques: acknowledge and reframe, redirect question, case study/proof. Objections should cover: price, timing, trust, need, competitors, and stalling. Format: table with columns "Objection / Technique / Response."

💡 Tip: Memorize one best response for each objection until it becomes second nature.

2.3

Business Proposal

Write a business proposal for [your product/service] for [company name or type]. Decision-maker: [title]. Their challenge: [what they want to solve]. Client budget: [if known]. Structure: one-page executive summary (why us, what we offer, expected result), solution description, phases and timeline, pricing (3 tiers), case studies (2-3 relevant ones), guarantees, next step. Tone: professional but jargon-free. Length: 3-4 pages.

💡 Tip: Tailor the case studies section to the specific client's industry.

2.4

Price Negotiation Script

I have an upcoming price negotiation with a client for [your product/service]. Our price: $[amount]. The client wants: $[amount or "a discount"]. My minimum margin: $[amount or %]. Write a negotiation scenario with a concession range. Include: 3 arguments defending the price, 4 compromise options without a direct discount (bonuses, terms, volume), "if...then" technique for each concession, bridging phrases between positions, red lines and how to signal them.

💡 Tip: Never give a discount without a reciprocal concession from the client.

2.5

Lead Qualification

Develop a lead qualification system for [your product]. Target customer: [target audience]. Average sales cycle: [duration]. Create: a scoring model (criteria and points), 10 qualifying questions with explanations of what each answer reveals, a priority matrix (hot/warm/cold), a marketing-to-sales handoff process, SLA for response time by lead type.

💡 Tip: Revisit the scoring model every quarter based on closed deal data.

2.6

Follow-Up Sequence

Write a 7-message follow-up sequence after a meeting/demo of [your product] with a potential client. Context: [meeting type, what was discussed]. Client: [title, company]. For each message, specify: day to send, channel (email/messenger/phone), message text, purpose of the touchpoint. The sequence should escalate in assertiveness without becoming spam. The last message should be a break-up email.

💡 Tip: Send the first follow-up within 2 hours of the meeting while the context is fresh.

2.7

Investor Pitch Deck

Create the structure and copy for a pitch deck to raise funding for [your product/company]. Stage: [pre-seed/seed/Series A]. Round size: $[amount]. Current metrics: [MRR, users, growth]. Slides: problem, solution, market (TAM/SAM/SOM), product, business model, traction, competition, team, financial plan, the ask. For each slide: headline, key message, what to show visually, speaker notes.

💡 Tip: Investors make their decision in the first 3 slides. Make them flawless.

2.8

Upsell Strategy

Develop an upsell and cross-sell system for [your product]. Current product lineup: [list products and prices]. Average order value: $[amount]. Current LTV: $[amount]. Describe: which products to offer at which stage, upsell triggers (customer actions, timing, events), scripts/messages for each scenario, target lift in AOV and LTV, sales team incentive structure for upselling.

💡 Tip: The best time to upsell is right after the customer achieves a result with the base product.

2.9

Win-Back Strategy

Write a win-back strategy for churned customers of [your product]. Suspected reasons for leaving: [reasons]. Number churned in [time period]: [number]. Average spend before churning: $[amount]. Develop: segmentation of churned customers (by reason, LTV, recency), communication sequence for each segment (3-5 touchpoints), special offers for reactivation, win-back call script, success metrics for the campaign.

💡 Tip: Customers with the highest former LTV will deliver the fastest ROI on a win-back campaign.

2.10

Inbound Call Script

Write a script for handling inbound calls/inquiries for [your product]. Lead source: [where leads come from]. Target audience: [target audience]. The script should include: greeting (5 seconds), need discovery (3-5 questions), budget and timeline qualification, benefit-driven solution presentation, scheduling the next step, handling "I need to think about it." Flag critical mistakes to avoid.

💡 Tip: An inbound lead is already interested. Don't "sell" them; help them make a decision.

2.11

Channel Sales

Develop a channel sales model for [your product]. Partner type: [integrators/resellers/affiliates]. Partner's target market: [description]. Create: value proposition for the partner (why it's profitable to sell us), financial terms (commission, bonuses, tiers), 7-day onboarding program for new partners, sales enablement materials, reporting and quality control system.

💡 Tip: Train partners to sell your product as well as your own sales team does.

2.12

Product Demo

Write a demo presentation script for [your product] for [target audience]. Duration: [number] minutes. Key customer pain point: [customer pain point]. Structure: hook (30 seconds), overview of the problem and current solutions, demonstration of 3 key features (from main pain to bonus features), audience interaction (2 questions), social proof (case study), offer and next step. For each block: timing, key phrases, what to show on screen.

💡 Tip: Show the result in the first 2 minutes, then explain how it works.

2.13

CRM Process Design

Design a sales pipeline in the CRM for [your product]. Lead sources: [channels]. Sales cycle: [duration]. Team: [number of reps]. Describe: pipeline stages (5-7) with transition criteria, required actions at each stage, automations (tasks, notifications, emails), fields to fill at each stage, manager reports (daily, weekly, monthly), rules for handling stalled deals.

💡 Tip: "Thinking about it" is not a pipeline stage. Replace it with a concrete action.

2.14

Price Justification

My product [your product] costs $[price], which is [number]% more expensive than competitors. Target audience: [target audience]. Build a price justification framework: 5 ways to defend a higher price, ROI calculator for the client (what inputs are needed, formula, example calculation), 3 "cheap vs. premium" case studies showing consequences, bridging phrases for price discussions, when to offer a discount (and when absolutely not).

💡 Tip: Shift the conversation from price to total cost of ownership and ROI.

2.15

RFP / Tender Response

Help me put together a winning RFP response for [tender description]. Client: [organization type]. Evaluation criteria: [if known]. Our advantage: [your product/expertise]. Write: cover letter, company description (focused on relevant experience), technical proposal (structure), pricing proposal (pricing strategy), "why us" section (5 arguments with evidence). Flag common mistakes in tender submissions.

💡 Tip: Study the client's previously awarded contracts if they're publicly available.

2.16

Selling Through Messaging Apps

Write 5 sales conversation scripts for [your product] via DM/messaging. Lead source: [where the lead came from]. Target audience: [target audience]. For each scenario: opening message (not "Hi, how can I help you?"), a chain of 5-8 messages, decision points (if client responds A vs. B), transition to payment/meeting, handling "too expensive" and "I'll think about it." Keep in mind that people read short messages in chat.

💡 Tip: One message in chat should be no longer than 3 lines, or they won't read it.

2.17

Enterprise Deal Strategy

Help me plan a strategy for closing a large deal. Client: [company]. Product: [your product]. Deal size: $[amount]. Decision-maker: [title]. Decision timeline: [duration]. Current stage: [description]. Create: influence map (who impacts the decision, their interests), touchpoint plan for each stakeholder, arguments for each level (user/manager/executive/finance), plan to neutralize an internal competitor, checklist for preparing for the final meeting.

💡 Tip: In enterprise deals, sell the business outcome in the language of each specific stakeholder, not the product.

2.18

Sales Rep Onboarding

Create a 30-day onboarding program for a new sales rep at [your product]. Product: [description]. Sales cycle: [duration]. Current team: [number] people. Split by week: week 1 (product and market), week 2 (processes and tools), week 3 (supervised practice), week 4 (independent work). For each day: tasks, materials, deliverable. KPIs for reaching full productivity.

💡 Tip: Have the new rep listen to 10 of the best call recordings on day one.

2.19

Accounts Receivable Management

Write a system for managing accounts receivable for [your business]. Standard payment terms: [number] days. Typical late payers: [client type]. Develop: a reminder sequence (from soft to firm, 6 stages with scripts), past-due phone call script, formal demand letter, installment plan terms, criteria for escalation to legal, preventive measures (how to avoid late payments in the first place).

💡 Tip: Send the first reminder 3 days before the due date, not after.

2.20

Seasonal Sales Strategy

Develop a year-round sales strategy for [your product] accounting for seasonality. Peak months: [months]. Slow months: [months]. Annual revenue target: $[amount]. For each quarter: revenue goal, key activities, offers, focus segments, team size. Separately: how to offset the dip in slow months (new segments, products, markets), sales team compensation structure adjusted for seasonality.

💡 Tip: Use the slow season to train the team and prepare for the peak.

3. Content & Copywriting

3.1

Monthly Content Plan

Create a 30-day content plan for [brand/product] in the [niche] space. Target audience: [target audience]. Content goal: [attract/engage/sell]. Channels: [blog/social media/newsletter]. For each day: topic, format (article/post/video/carousel), key message, CTA, hashtags (if social). Content mix: 40% educational, 30% engaging, 20% promotional, 10% entertaining. Topics should not repeat.

💡 Tip: Batch your content: create an entire week's content in a single session.

3.2

Long-Form Blog Post

Write the structure and full text of a blog article on the topic "[article topic]." Target keyword: [SEO keyword]. Target audience: [target audience]. Length: 2,000-3,000 words. Structure: H1 headline (with keyword), introduction with a hook, 5-7 H2 sections with H3 subsections, practical examples in each section, conclusion with CTA. Tone: authoritative yet accessible. Insert 3 spots for internal links and 2 for external authoritative sources.

💡 Tip: Start with the most valuable section so readers get value even if they only skim.

3.3

AIDA Sales Copy

Write sales copy for [your product] using the AIDA formula. Target audience: [target audience]. Primary pain point: [customer pain point]. Result the customer gets: [result]. Price: $[price]. Attention: provocative headline and opening paragraph. Interest: unpack the problem so readers see themselves (3 scenarios). Desire: show the solution and results (facts, numbers, case study). Action: offer with a deadline and bonus. Length: 1,500-2,000 words.

💡 Tip: Read the copy aloud. If you stumble on a phrase, rewrite it simpler.

3.4

Brand Storytelling

Write a brand story for [brand] for the "About Us" page. Facts: founder [name], year founded [year], niche [niche], mission [mission]. Key milestone: [event]. Use the structure: hero (founder) faced a problem, made a decision, went through hardship, built a solution, now helps others. Tone: sincere, no grandstanding. The story should build trust and explain why the company does what it does. Length: 500-700 words.

💡 Tip: A real story with setbacks generates more trust than a perfect one.

3.5

Launch Warm-Up Post Series

Write a series of 7 posts to warm up the audience before launching [your product]. Launch day: [date]. Target audience: [target audience]. Platform: [social network]. Sequence: 1) The problem (pain), 2) Why existing solutions fail, 3) Insight/new approach, 4) My story and expertise, 5) Social proof, 6) Full announcement with details, 7) Sales open with a deadline. For each post: text, visual description, CTA.

💡 Tip: Start the warm-up 7-10 days before launch, no later.

3.6

Customer Case Study

Write a case study of [your product] used by a customer. Customer: [company type/person]. Starting situation: [problem]. What was done: [actions]. Result: [numbers]. Timeframe: [duration]. Structure: headline with the result in numbers, summary (3 lines), situation before, challenges and constraints, solution (why they chose us), implementation process, results (with charts/numbers), customer quote, next steps. Format: ready to publish on the website.

💡 Tip: The most persuasive case study contains specific "before" and "after" numbers.

3.7

Product Descriptions for Catalog

Write compelling product descriptions for 5 items in the [product category]. Target audience: [target audience]. Platform: [online store/Amazon/Shopify]. For each product [insert names]: keyword-rich title, short description (2-3 sentences for preview), full description (200-300 words) focused on benefits, 5 bullet points with features (feature -> benefit), SEO meta description. Avoid the words "quality," "unique," "best."

💡 Tip: Describe the product through a usage scenario, not dry specs.

3.8

Video Script

Write a video script for [purpose: ad/tutorial/review] that runs [number] minutes. Product: [your product]. Target audience: [target audience]. Platform: [YouTube/Reels/TikTok]. Script format: timecode, on-screen action, voiceover/dialogue, captions/graphics. Hook in the first 3 seconds. If Reels/TikTok: vertical format, fast cuts, trending elements. If YouTube: re-engage attention every 30 seconds.

💡 Tip: Film the hook three different ways and pick the best one based on retention data.

3.9

White Paper / Research Report

Create the structure and key talking points for a white paper on "[topic]" by [brand]. Target audience: [target audience, e.g., mid-market CEOs]. Goal: [lead generation/thought leadership]. Structure: cover with headline, table of contents, executive summary, problem statement with market data, analysis of current approaches, proposed solution, case studies (3), forecasts and recommendations, about the author/company. Length: 15-20 pages. For each section: key points and data to source.

💡 Tip: A white paper works as a lead magnet only if it contains original data or insights.

3.10

Rewrite and Improve Existing Copy

Here is my text: [paste text]. Target audience: [target audience]. Goal: [sell/inform/engage]. Rewrite it, improving: structure (add subheadings, bullets, paragraphs no longer than 3 lines), readability (simplify complex sentences, remove jargon), persuasiveness (replace vague claims with specifics, switch passive voice to active), SEO (naturally weave in keywords [list]). Show changes with explanations.

💡 Tip: A good rewrite cuts the text by 20-30% without losing meaning.

3.11

Headlines and Subheadlines

Generate 30 headline options for [content type: article/landing page/ad] on the topic "[topic]." Target audience: [target audience]. Group by formula: numbers ("7 Ways to..."), question ("Why Does Your...?"), how-to ("How to Increase..."), provocation, benefit, fear of missing out, curiosity. For each headline, rate: clickability (1-10), audience fit (1-10). Highlight the top 5 for A/B testing.

💡 Tip: The headline drives 80% of content performance. Spend as much time on it as you do on the body copy.

3.12

Tone of Voice Guide

Develop a Tone of Voice guide for [brand]. Niche: [niche]. Target audience: [target audience]. Brand values: [values]. Describe: brand voice (3-4 adjectives with explanations), how we speak vs. how we DON'T speak (table), sample copy in our style for different channels (website, social media, email, support), brand vocabulary (marker words and banned words), rules for adapting tone by context.

💡 Tip: Give the guide to 3 different writers and check if they produce consistent output.

3.13

UX Microcopy

Write UI copy for [product type: app/website/SaaS]. Target audience: [target audience]. Needed: CTA buttons (10 variants for different screens), error messages (5 scenarios), empty states (3 screens), onboarding tooltips (5 steps), notifications (push and email triggers, 7 total), placeholder text for forms. Each piece: short (under 50 characters for buttons, under 150 for messages), instantly clear, consistent brand tone.

💡 Tip: Test UI copy on people unfamiliar with the product. If they don't get it instantly, rewrite.

3.14

Press Release

Write a press release about [event: product launch/partnership/milestone]. Company: [brand]. Date: [date]. Target media: [type of outlets]. Structure: headline (fact + newsworthiness), subheadline (expansion), dateline, lead paragraph (who, what, when, where, why), 2-3 detail paragraphs, executive quote, partner/customer quote (if applicable), company boilerplate, press contact. Style: factual, no advertising language.

💡 Tip: Send press releases to journalists between 10:00-11:00 AM on Tuesday through Thursday for maximum pickup.

3.15

FAQ Content

Build an expanded FAQ for [your product/website]. Target audience: [target audience]. Compile 25 questions by category: about the product (7), payment and pricing (5), delivery/setup (5), usage (5), warranties and returns (3). For each question: answer (2-4 sentences), SEO keyword, internal link (where to direct). Write questions the way real people ask them, not how lawyers write them.

💡 Tip: Mine real questions from support chat and customer reviews instead of making them up.

3.16

Podcast Content

Plan 10 podcast episodes on the topic of [podcast topic] for [target audience]. Format: [interview/solo/discussion]. Duration: [number] minutes. For each episode: title, short description (3 sentences), 5-7 discussion questions/talking points, guest type (if interview), key takeaway for the listener, closing CTA. The first 3 episodes should cover fundamentals; the rest should go deeper.

💡 Tip: Record podcasts in batches of 3-4 episodes per session.

3.17

Website Glossary

Create a glossary of 30 terms for a website in the [niche] space. Target audience: [target audience, their knowledge level]. For each term: definition (1-2 sentences in plain language), usage example, related terms, SEO target keyword. Arrange alphabetically. Each term should function as a standalone mini-article that drives informational SEO traffic.

💡 Tip: A glossary delivers steady informational traffic with minimal update costs.

3.18

Conference Speech

Write a speech/talk on "[topic]" for a presentation at [event name/conference type]. Audience: [target audience, number of people]. Duration: [number] minutes. Structure: strong opener (story/fact/question), 3 key sections (thesis -> argument -> example -> takeaway for each), transitions between sections, closing with a call to action. Style: conversational, with pauses. Mark spots for slides and moments of audience interaction.

💡 Tip: Rehearse with a timer. One slide per 2 minutes, or you'll run over.

3.19

Webinar Script

Write a full script for a selling webinar on "[topic]" to sell [your product]. Target audience: [target audience]. Duration: 60-90 minutes. Structure by minute: 0-5 welcome and promise, 5-25 content section (3 insights), 25-35 case studies, 35-45 product presentation, 45-55 offer and bonuses, 55-65 Q&A, 65-70 close with deadline. For each block: what to say, what to show on screen, interaction points (polls, chat prompts).

💡 Tip: The highest webinar conversion happens in the first 15 minutes after presenting the offer.

3.20

Quarterly Content Strategy

Develop a 3-month content strategy for [brand] in the [niche] space. Goals: [goals, e.g., 50% traffic growth, 100 leads/month]. Current state: [traffic, followers, conversion]. Include: content gap analysis (what competitors have that we don't), content pyramid (pillar content -> clusters -> micro-content), publishing calendar by channel, KPIs per content type, repurposing system (1 long-form article = 5 posts = 3 stories = 1 video), resources (who, hours, design budget).

💡 Tip: One strong long-form piece per week beats five weak posts every day.

4. SEO & Traffic

4.1

SEO Strategy for a New Site
